Method

How does this work?

We learn the system and the needs of its users. We create small, simple tools and push them out to users quickly to test their viability and utility. If a tool is useful, it will be used and merit further development.

Why do this?

Current Government procurement methods can be slow and overly complex. This makes for heavy, complex, and difficult-to-maintain technology.

Why does this work better than the status quo?

Iterative methods make the software development process part of the research. Features and development are data-driven, meaning use drives development. This leads to lean, well-scoped, and proven software, shipped piecemeal in response to user needs.
